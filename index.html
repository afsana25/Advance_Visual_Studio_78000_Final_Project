The US Choropleth Map Based on Poverty Rate

How Education Shapes the Income and Poverty Rate of The US?

Afsana Mimi

Introduction

The United States is one of the wealthiest countries globally, but it still has poverty prevails in its states. The project's motivation is to find the relationship between the states' poverty rate, per capita income, and education scores via linear Regression and Correlation. The visualization involves choropleth map visualizations, a series of the Linear Regression includes Education Score Vs. Per Capita Income, Per Capita Vs. Poverty Rate and Education Score Vs. Per Capita. The choropleth map of the US states shows the highest to lowest poverty rate with color-coded light with blue, orange, and green based on poverty criteria(High, Middle, Low). The Regression shows how to fit the Education score, Poverty rate, and per capita income to their perspective regression line. A table chart shows the correlations between the indicators, Poverty rate, Education Score, and Per Capita Income in the HTML table, with red as the inverse relationship and green as the positive relationship.

Methodology

Data Collection and Manipulation:

All spreadsheet data have been collected from the US Census and worldpopulationreview.com. The Poverty rate and education score have been collected From worldpopulationreview.com. The per capita income has been collected from the US census in excel format. Most of the data is combined and cleaned(null values or drop columns) in SQL and also completed in excel. The US state geographic data has been collected in GEO-JSON format and manually added the indicators such as per capita income, education score, and poverty rate.


Map Visulization:

The choropleth map has been completed in javascript using the d3 with version 6. Based on the criteria of the poverty group, color has been applied to the choropleth map. The blue color represents high poverty, the orange color represents middle poverty, and the green color represents Lower poverty rates within the states. The hover of the map shows information about the poverty rate, per capita income, and education score. The geo JSON data file has used to complete the map.


Linear Rigressions:

All the Regression between indicators has been completed via the D3(3.17) javascript. The straight line shows the regression line between the indicators and hovers over the line presenting the computed value for the predicted model( Y-axis or Y as the predicted indicator, and x-axis or X as the input value). According to the choropleth map, the legend is also set up as blue for high poverty, orange for middle poverty, and green for lower poverty. All the regression visuals show the equation( y = ax+ b, a is the coefficient for x value, and b is the intercept) and R^2 value(Co-efficient determination, A Fitted value to predict the model). The first two regression lines show the indicator's inverse relationship with the inverse regression line(upward left, a person's front screen view). The last one shows the positive relationship with an increased line to the upward right(a person's front screen view).

Correlation:

R software has used to complete the correlation. The negative value means they have an inverse relationship, and the positive value means they have a positive relationship. The value, 0.60 or more points, they have a strong relationship. The red color represents a negative relationship in this table, and the green color represents a positive relationship between the variables.

Linear Rigression of Education Score VS Poverty Rate

The above regression chart shows a negative relationship between the education score and the poverty rate. The negative value before x coefficients also implies the relationship. The R2 = 0.746 tells that almost 75% of the points fit to the regression line and that it is an excellent model to predict the education score based on the poverty rate. The circle is present in the per capita income squared root multiplied by 0.001.

Linear Rigression of Per Capita VS Poverty Rate

The second regression shows a negative relationship between the per capita income and the poverty rate as the negative value before x coefficients. The R2 = 0.4413 states that almost 44% of the data fit the regression line, which is a moderately good model for predicting per capita income based on the poverty rate. The radius of the circle shows the education score with a squared root value.

Linear Rigression of Education Score Vs Per Capita

The last regression chart shows a positive relationship between the education score and the per capita income as the positive value before x coefficients implies the same thing as well. The R2 = 0.6167 states that almost 62% of the data fit the regression line, which is a good model for predicting the education score based on the per capita income. The circle's size demonstrates the poverty rate multiplied by 1000 to show the existence of the points.

Correlation

The above correlation is easily showing the Poverty rate is negatively correlated to education score and per capita income, and they are strongly correlated. It means that if one is increasing other one is decreasing. We also see that education score and per capita income positively correlate with a firm number, indicating that one is rising and the other is rising as well.

Conclusion

The analyses show the different indicator relationships in terms of Poverty rate, education score, and per capita of United states. From the above analysis, we can easily find that the Education rate and per capita income have a negative impact on the Poverty rate. The relationship between education scores and per capita of each state is highly positive. In Conclusion, we can state that education positively impacts per capita income and negatively affects the Poverty rate.
